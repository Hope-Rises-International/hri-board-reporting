# Hope Rises International — Technology & Innovation Report
**Prepared for the Board of Directors | March 2026**

---

## What We're Building — and Why It Matters

Over the past two months, Hope Rises International has quietly built something remarkable: a suite of intelligent systems that handle the repetitive operational work that used to consume hundreds of staff hours every month. We did it without hiring developers, without buying expensive software platforms, and without disrupting day-to-day operations.

Using an AI assistant called Claude Code — directed in plain English by our own team — we've deployed **15 projects** across five major areas of operations. The results so far speak for themselves, and the roadmap ahead is even more promising.

## What's Already Working

**Intelligent Email Management** — An AI system reviews the CEO's inbox four times daily, automatically sorting routine messages, forwarding receipts to our expense system, and surfacing only what truly needs human attention. What once took an hour a day now takes five minutes.

**Donor Portfolio Intelligence** — Our fundraising team now has a real-time window into 3,185 donor relationships. Instead of pulling manual reports, they can instantly see who needs attention, which donors are growing or declining, and what their next best action should be — all updated daily from Salesforce.

**Campaign Scorecards** — Every direct mail appeal is now automatically scored and classified, helping us quickly see which campaigns to repeat, which to rethink, and where our fundraising dollars work hardest.

**Tax Receipt Generation** — A process that took 10–15 minutes per donor receipt now takes 30 seconds. Staff search a name, click a button, and get a print-ready receipt on official letterhead.

**Timesheet & Payroll Processing** — A new web-based system replaces our spreadsheet timesheets for all 20+ staff, handling approvals, PTO tracking, reminders, and payroll file generation automatically.

**Financial Data Infrastructure** — General ledger data from Sage Intacct now flows into a central repository that powers income statements, budget-to-actual comparisons, and grant reporting — laying the groundwork for fully automated board financial reports.

## What's Ahead

A comprehensive review of every team member's recurring work revealed that **approximately 455 hours per month** — over a quarter of our total operational labor — can be shifted from manual data entry to automated systems. We've organized this into three waves:

- **Wave 1 (Now):** Automated financial reporting, expense processing, and HR onboarding workflows
- **Wave 2 (Mid-Year):** AI-assisted donor research, advanced Salesforce automation, and partner reporting
- **Wave 3 (Fall):** A unified operations dashboard giving leadership a single view across all systems

## What This Means for the Mission

Every hour our staff spends copying data between spreadsheets or sorting emails is an hour not spent serving leprosy patients, supporting displaced families, or building relationships with the donors who make that work possible.

This initiative is not about replacing people — it's about freeing them. Our finance team becomes analysts instead of data entry clerks. Our fundraisers spend time with donors instead of pulling reports. Our program staff focus on outcomes instead of paperwork.

We've also eliminated a critical organizational risk: every system we build is fully documented and self-tracking. The knowledge of how things work no longer lives in one person's head — it lives in systems anyone on the team can maintain.
