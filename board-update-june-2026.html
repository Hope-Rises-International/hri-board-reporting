A Legacy of Service • Retirement Tribute
Honoring Dr. Paul Saunderson — 26 Years of Quiet, Faithful Impact

This month we celebrate Dr. Paul Saunderson, who retires at the close of the fiscal year after 26 years with Hope Rises International. Paul joined the organization in 2000 as Medical Director and most recently served as a technical advisor, capping a remarkable 45-year career in leprosy that began almost the very month the World Health Organization introduced multidrug therapy (MDT).

Reflecting on those decades, Paul named the global rollout of MDT as the single greatest change he has witnessed — “one of the few programs in which every patient in the world gets free treatment from the same source.” He also pointed the way forward, to prevention: Hope Rises (then American Leprosy Missions) helped fund both frontiers of that work — the landmark post-exposure prophylaxis trial and the LepVax vaccine — when few others were willing to take the plunge.

But as his colleagues reminded us, a CV does not capture the man. Paul came to this work not as a career but as a calling. A voracious reader with an encyclopedic mind, he served the poorest, the most stigmatized, and the most forgotten — decade after decade, not with fanfare, but with competence, presence, and the quiet conviction that their suffering mattered to God.

“Paul does not win people over through charm or social energy. He wins them over the slow way — through rigor and credibility, and the kind of presence that can only be built through a lifetime of showing up.” — Jim Oehrig, on his colleague of many years

The staff gathered to honor Paul with words of gratitude and a liturgy for one who is retiring. We invite the board to share in that moment.

From Board Chair Neal Joseph • Strategic Spotlight
LepVax: A Pivotal Funding Opportunity

At the request of our board chair, Neal Joseph, this update includes the Executive Summary from the recent LepVax webinar — chaired by Dr. Paul Saunderson — shared for the benefit of any board members who were unable to attend. Neal noted that it offers a strong, high-level overview of the leprosy vaccine’s funding, strategic importance, and next steps. LepVax is one of the most promising leprosy-specific vaccine candidates in development, designed to do what multidrug therapy cannot: interrupt transmission and reduce the nerve damage that causes lifelong disability.

Having completed its first human safety study with strong results and secured regulatory approval for the next phase in Brazil, the program stands at a pivotal point. The principal obstacle is no longer scientific feasibility — it is financial. Closing the remaining funding gap would unlock manufacturing and launch the decisive Phase 1b trial.

Fundraising & Partnerships
Momentum Heading Into Year-End

Our fiscal year-end newsletter and email campaign are now in market during one of the most important giving seasons of the year. Engagement is climbing — email open rates doubled across our May sends, and we are now searched more than 30,000 times a month through Google search under both “American Leprosy Missions” and “Hope Rises International,” a strong sign that supporters increasingly recognize us as one and the same. Our visibility in AI-powered search continues to grow as well, positioning Hope Rises as a trusted authority on neglected tropical diseases.

On the grants front, the team is building a repeatable engine — refining a master funder list, pursuing active applications such as the Crowell Trust (supported by faith-forward letters of recommendation from ministry partners), and cultivating relationships with invitation-only funders.

Among major donors, a couple who had previously stepped away from giving has re-engaged in a significant way — opening a donor-advised fund and committing at least $10,000 a year for the next five years. Long-standing church relationships are deepening too, including Hope of the World — a partner of 14 years — and Fellowship Greenville. These are not transactional relationships; they are genuine partnerships in the work God is doing.
