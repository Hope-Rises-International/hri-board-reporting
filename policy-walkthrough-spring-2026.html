How This Works

Each section covers one sub-policy from the Spring monitoring set. Key Evidence shows the abbreviated interpretation—what the Board looks for. Discussion Prompts appear on policies rated Medium or Discussion to help generate conversation.

Easy policies show key evidence and a True/False footer only—no discussion prompts needed. Full policy text is in the monitoring reports on each board member's screen for reference.

Everything discussed gets recorded, minuted, and presented as a policy monitoring evaluation.

Policy 4.2

Board Job Description

Board-Management Connection
The job of the Board is to represent God and those who share a demonstrated commitment to HRI's stated Ends in determining and demanding appropriate organizational performance.
3 sub-policies in this group
4.2.1
Board as Link to Ownership
Medium
The Board is the link between the organization and the Ownership. This policy ensures the Board maintains periodic and appropriate engagement with those who share a demonstrated commitment to HRI's Ends.

Key Evidence

  • Owner perspectives gathered through any channel and presented to the Board
  • Board engagement with Owners through events, field visits, or outreach
  • Board deliberation on at least one issue that expressly considered Owner values (reflected in minutes)
  • Standard: periodic and appropriate engagement—at least one of the above

Discussion Prompts

  • Did any of these evidence criteria actually happen this year? Which ones?
  • Are there gaps in Owner engagement that the Board should address going forward?
True or false? Does the CEO have evidence? Does the board member have evidence?
4.2.2
Policy Addresses All Four Categories
Easy
Board Policy must address each category of organizational decision: Ends (what good, for whom, at what cost), Executive Limitations (constraints on executive authority), Governance Process (how the Board operates), and Board/CEO Linkage (how power is delegated and monitored).

Key Evidence

  • Policy manual is current and covers all four categories (Ends, ELs, GP, B/CEO Linkage)
  • Annual review cycle completed by fiscal year-end; change log on file
  • Monitoring reports and minutes demonstrate active policy use
True or false? Evidence to support?
4.2.3
Board Monitors CEO Performance
Easy
The Board actively monitors CEO performance, measured by compliance with Ends policy (4.2.2.1) and Executive Limitations (4.2.2.2). This ensures accountability flows through the policy framework, not ad hoc evaluation.

Key Evidence

  • Board-approved monitoring schedule completed for the year
  • Any exceptions or non-compliance findings noted and addressed
  • Monitoring reports on file and accepted by the Board
True or false? Evidence to support?
Policy 4.3

Agenda Planning

Board Process
To do its job effectively, the Board will follow an agenda that completes a review of its Policy annually and continually improves Board performance through Board education and enriched input and deliberation.
7 sub-policies in this group
4.3.1
Board Follows Annual Policy Review Agenda
Medium
The policy review cycle concludes each year as of the last day of the fiscal year (June 30) so that administrative planning and budgeting can be based on accomplishing a one-year segment of the Board's most recent statement of Ends and Executive Limitations.

Key Evidence

  • Annual policy review plan approved; completion tracked by FY end (June 30)
  • Board education plan for the year (topics and dates)
  • Change log entries for revised or affirmed policies

Discussion Prompts

  • Is the review cycle on track to complete by June 30? Any gaps?
  • What governance education was offered this year, and was it sufficient?
True or false? Does the CEO have evidence? Does the board member have evidence?
4.3.2
Agenda Development Starts in Spring
Medium
The review cycle starts with the Board's development of its agenda for the following year. Methods of gaining Ownership input and governance education are assembled during spring. The Chair prepares and circulates a tentative agenda; directors are encouraged to recommend matters for Board consideration at least 20 days prior to scheduled meetings.

Key Evidence

  • Preliminary FY agenda and monitoring schedule developed in spring; finalized in fall
  • Owner-input and Board education components included in the plan
  • Chair's tentative agenda circulated; director input received ≥20 days prior

Discussion Prompts

  • Has next year's agenda development begun? Who is leading it?
  • Are there gaps in how Owner input or governance education feeds into agenda planning?
True or false? Does the CEO have evidence? Does the board member have evidence?
4.3.3
Board Meets 3x/Year, Once in Spring
Easy
The Board will generally meet three times a year: once in the spring, once in the fall (annual meeting), and once via teleconference. Additional meetings may be scheduled as required.

Key Evidence

  • Spring meeting held [date]; fall annual meeting held [date]; teleconference held [date]
  • Any additional meetings duly called and minuted
True or false? Evidence to support?
4.3.4
CEO Monitoring on the Agenda

CEO monitoring will be included on the agenda, particularly if monitoring reports show policy violations or if policy criteria are to be debated. This ensures the Board's oversight role is explicit in every meeting agenda.

Key Evidence

  • Are monitoring reports getting meaningful deliberation time, or are they routine sign-offs?
True or false? Does the CEO have evidence? Does the board member have evidence?

4.3.6
Board Monitors/Amends Policy; Specific EL Cross-References
Discussion
Without limiting the Board's right to monitor and amend any part of its Policy, the Board shall consider certain specific issues that might arise under EL policies 2.3.2, 2.3.3, and 2.10.2. This creates a standing trigger to review specific executive limitations when relevant issues surface.

Key Evidence

  • CEO updates screened each meeting for triggering issues under 2.3.2, 2.3.3, 2.10.2
  • If triggered, item placed on agenda; otherwise, minutes note no triggering issues
  • Highlights EL policies 2.3.2–2.3.3 and 2.10.2

Discussion Prompts

  • Are these EL cross-references (2.3.2, 2.3.3, 2.10.2) still worth highlighting, or have they become irrelevant?
  • Has the Board needed to exercise its right to amend policy outside the regular review cycle?
True or false? Does the CEO have evidence? Does the board member have evidence?

Reserve/Endowment Transfers

Key Evidence

  • Cross-references EL 2.5.9 and 2.6.2 regarding reserve access limits

Policy 4.4

Chairperson's Role

Chair Governance
The Chairperson assures the integrity of the Board's process and, secondarily, represents the Board to outside parties when necessary or appropriate.
3 sub-policies in this group
4.4.1
Chair Ensures Board Follows Its Own Rules
Easy
The Chair's job is to assure the Board acts consistently with its own rules and those legitimately imposed from outside. Discussion is limited to Board-level issues (not CEO territory), and deliberation must be fair, open, thorough, timely, and orderly.

Key Evidence

  • Chair attests meetings stayed within Board authority and governance scope
  • Minutes confirm no operational drift into CEO/staff territory
  • Board members affirm fair, open, and orderly facilitation
  • External stakeholder interactions followed Board-stated positions
True or false? Evidence to support?
4.4.2
Chair Leads Governance Decisions and Representation
Easy
The Chair leads Board decisions on Governance Process and Board/CEO Linkage topics. The Chair is empowered to facilitate meetings (ruling, recognizing) but has no individual authority over Ends or EL policy, and therefore no individual authority to supervise or direct the CEO. The Chair may represent the Board externally on Board-stated positions.

Key Evidence

  • Minutes reflect proper facilitation (recognizing speakers, ruling on motions)
  • No instances of unilateral Ends/EL policy changes or CEO supervision by Chair
  • External communications match Board-stated positions
True or false? Evidence to support?
4.4.3
Ad Hoc Committees; CEO Succession Readiness
Easy
The Chair may appoint directors and a chairperson for special task force or ad hoc committees. In the event of a need for CEO succession, the Chair will immediately call the board into action and nominate a succession committee to advance a plan agreed upon by the board.

Key Evidence

  • Ad hoc committees: none formed this year (or list with scope and timeline)
  • CEO emergency succession protocol: current and on file, reviewed annually
True or false? Evidence to support?
Policy 4.5

Board Code of Conduct

Directors' Conduct
The Board commits itself and its directors to lawful, biblical, ethical, and businesslike conduct, including proper use of authority and appropriate decorum when acting as Board directors.
5 sub-policies in this group
4.5.1
Un-conflicted Loyalty; Annual COI Attestation
Medium
Directors must have un-conflicted loyalty to the Ends of the organization. This accountability supersedes any conflicting loyalty to advocacy or interest groups, membership on other boards or staffs, and personal interests. Board directors will annually attest to compliance.

Key Evidence

  • COI attestations received: count/percent by director (target: 100%)
  • Disclosure log entries (who/what/date) and management steps taken
  • Minutes showing recusals where conflicts existed
  • No substantiated loyalty conflicts

Discussion Prompts

  • Are all COI statements on file? Any outstanding?
  • Were there any situations this year where conflicting loyalties needed to be managed?
True or false? Does the CEO have evidence? Does the board member have evidence?
4.5.2
Conflict of Interest; No Self-Dealing; Recusal
Easy
Directors must avoid conflict of interest with respect to fiduciary responsibility. No self-dealing or private business between directors and the organization. When a director has an unavoidable conflict, they must absent themselves from both deliberation and vote. Directors must not use their positions to obtain employment for themselves, family, or close associates.

Key Evidence

  • Related-party/procurement log: any director-linked vendors handled transparently
  • Recusal tracker: director, matter, date; confirmation of absenting from discussion and vote
  • No director or close associate employment by HRI
True or false? Evidence to support?
4.5.3
Directors Do Not Exercise Individual Authority
Medium
Board directors may not attempt to exercise individual authority over HRI or the staff except as explicitly set forth in Board policies. Directors' interactions with the CEO/staff must recognize the lack of individual authority. Interactions with the public must recognize that no director speaks for the Board except on explicitly-stated Board decisions. Outside of appropriately called Board discussion, directors will give no consequence or voice to individual judgments of CEO performance.

Key Evidence

  • Zero substantiated instances of unauthorized direction of staff or CEO
  • External remarks align with Board-approved positions or spokesperson protocol
  • CEO evaluation occurs only through Board process; no individual judgments expressed outside meetings

Discussion Prompts

  • Can both the CEO and the Chair attest that no individual director exercised unauthorized authority?
  • Should the CEO be present for this discussion to provide a candid perspective?
True or false? Does the CEO have evidence? Does the board member have evidence?
4.5.4
Confidentiality
Easy
Board directors will respect the confidentiality appropriate to issues of a sensitive nature. Directors will sign the Confidentiality Policy upon election.

Key Evidence

  • Confidentiality acknowledgments: 100% on file
  • Breach log: zero incidents (most years: one-line confirmation)
True or false? Evidence to support?
4.5.5
Dispute Resolution Policy
Easy
In the event of a dispute between directors, officers, or executive staff, the parties should follow the Dispute Resolution Policy (DRP) for mediation or subsequent arbitration. Directors will sign the DRP upon election.

Key Evidence

  • DRP acknowledgments: 100% on file
  • Dispute cases: none (most years: one-line confirmation)
True or false? Evidence to support?
Policy 4.6

Individual Responsibilities

Directors' Individual Commitments
The Board commits itself to the individual and collective participation of its members to ensure organizational success. Each Board director is expected to participate in the following ways.
8 sub-policies in this group
4.6.1
Meeting Attendance
Data Needed
Attendance at Board meetings is required. Board deliberation, decision-making, and monitoring are processes that require wholeness, collaboration, and participation. Directors who miss more than two meetings during any three-year term may be subject to removal from the Board.

Key Evidence

  • FY2026 attendance table by meeting (Spring, Fall, Teleconference) and by director
  • Board average attendance (target: ≥90%)
  • Term-level absence tracker: any director approaching/exceeding two absences
  • Attendance data to be compiled before the meeting

Discussion Prompts

  • Is attendance an issue? Are any directors approaching the two-absence threshold?
  • Does the NGC need to have a proactive conversation with any director about attendance?
True or false? Does the CEO have evidence? Does the board member have evidence?
4.6.2
Preparation, Participation, and Punctuality
Discussion
Board directors will prepare for meetings and participate productively in discussions, always within the boundaries of discipline established by the Board. Each member will contribute knowledge, skills, and experience.

Key Evidence

  • Board packets sent ≥7 days in advance of each meeting
  • Minutes reflect engaged, orderly discussion on policy monitoring/Ends/strategy
  • No pattern of preparation gaps, tardiness, or early departures

Discussion Prompts

  • Is the current "email to CEO—read, no questions / have questions" process enough to demonstrate preparation?
  • Are there directors who consistently seem underprepared, and how should that be addressed?
True or false? Does the CEO have evidence? Does the board member have evidence?
4.6.3
CEO Accountable to Board as Whole; Collegial Relationship
Data Needed
The CEO is accountable only to the Board as a whole, and not to individual Board directors. The relationship between the CEO and individual directors, including the Board Chair, is collegial, not hierarchical.

Key Evidence

  • Minutes confirm CEO evaluation handled in Board context (Spring)
  • No instances of individual direction/evaluation of the CEO
  • Cross-references 4.5.3 for individual authority data; 4.1.6 for one-voice data

Discussion Prompts

  • Can we confirm that no individual director attempted to direct or evaluate the CEO outside the Board process?
  • Is the anonymized contribution report ready for the meeting?
True or false? Does the CEO have evidence? Does the board member have evidence?

Policy 4.7

Board Committee Principles

Committee Operating Norms
Board committees, when used, will be assigned so as to reinforce the wholeness of the Board's job and so as never to interfere with delegation from the Board to the CEO.
6 sub-policies in this group
4.7.1
Committees Support Board Governance, Not Staff
Easy
Board committees are to help the Board do its job, never to help or advise the staff. Committees ordinarily assist the Board by preparing policy alternatives and implications for Board deliberation. They will normally not have direct dealings with current staff operations.

Key Evidence

  • Committees operated within governance scope; no operational advising
  • Committee roster and scope statements on file
  • Most years: one-line confirmation
True or false? Evidence to support?
4.7.2
No Unauthorized Committee Action for the Board
Easy
Board committees may not speak or act for the Board except when formally given such authority for specific and time-limited purposes. Expectations and authority will be carefully stated in order not to conflict with authority delegated to the CEO.

Key Evidence

  • No committee delegations (or list with scope/timeline if any)
  • Any authority to speak/act is narrowly scoped, time-limited, and Board-authorized
  • Most years: brief confirmation
True or false? Evidence to support?
4.7.3
Committees Cannot Exercise Authority Over Staff
Easy
Because the CEO works for the full Board, the CEO will not be expected to obtain approval of a Board committee before taking an executive action. Committees have no operational authority over staff.

Key Evidence

  • No committee directed staff this year
  • CEO did not seek committee pre-approval for executive actions
  • One-line confirmation most years
True or false? Evidence to support?
4.7.4
Committees Don't Monitor Areas They Created Policy For
Easy
Committees should avoid over-identification with organizational parts rather than the whole. A committee that helped the Board create policy on a topic will not be used to monitor organizational performance on that same subject.

Key Evidence

  • Confirmed via policy-origin vs. monitor assignment review
  • Monitoring remains a full-Board function via standard methods
  • Most years: one statement or brief table
True or false? Evidence to support?
4.7.5
Committees Used Sparingly; Ad Hoc with Sunset Dates
Easy
Committees will be used sparingly, ordinarily in an ad hoc capacity, and will exist only for a specified purpose and time period, other than the Board Standing Committees as set forth in Policy 4.8.

Key Evidence

  • Standing committees: NGC, Audit (ongoing)
  • Ad hoc committees: list with start/end dates (or "none active")
  • Only necessary committees formed; clear scope, deliverables, and sunset dates
True or false? Evidence to support?
4.7.6
Policy Applies to All Board-Formed Groups
Easy
This policy applies to any group formed by Board action, whether or not it is called a committee and regardless of whether the group includes Board members. It does not apply to committees formed under the authority of the CEO.

Key Evidence

  • Any Board-formed groups (task forces, working groups, advisory panels) followed 4.7 principles
  • CEO-formed management groups remain operational and separate from Board governance
  • Most years: "No additional Board-formed groups beyond standing committees"
True or false? Evidence to support?
